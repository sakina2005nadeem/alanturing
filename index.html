ABOUT ME

My name is Alan Turing (as you might already know), but my full name is Alan Mathison Turing (did you know that?). I was born on June 23, 1912, in Maida Vale, London and I am a British mathematician and logician, who has made major contributions to mathematics, cryptanalysis, logic, philosophy, and mathematical biology.

QUALIFICATION

I did my primary education at Hazelhurst Preparatory School, following which, I attended Sherborne School. After graduating from King's College, Cambridge, with a first-class honours degree, I was elected a Fellow of King's College at the age of 22. I went on to study mathematics at Princeton University, New Jersey, obtaining a Ph. D.

×

Turing Machine

In 1936, two years after completing my undergraduate degree, I introduced the concept of Turing Machine in my paper, "On computable numbers, with an application to the Entscheidungsproblem." This has since become the main abstract model of computation used by computer scientists.

Enigma Code

During the Second World War, I made several vital contributions as part of the British team trying to decipher intercepted German communications, which were encoded using specialized machines and had been thought unbreakable.

The Idea of a Computer

Immediately after the war, I designed an electronic computer, the ACE, for the National Physical Laboratory. A series of machines based on the design were eventually built, including one of the first commercial computer models.

Officer of the Order of the British Empire

I was appointed an Officer of the Order of the British Empire in 1946 by King George VI for waritime services.

Experience

I was a leading participant in the breaking of German ciphers at Bletchley Park. I've also worked part-time with the Government Code and Cipher School (GC & GS). I have also worked with US Navy cryptanalysts on the naval Enigma and bombe construction in Washington.
